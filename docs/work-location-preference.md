## Work Location Preference

Remote work is generally the best fit for James because it reduces avoidable friction and supports sustained independent problem-solving.

James also prefers remote or hybrid roles for health, sensory, schedule, and mobility reasons. He has self-disclosed AuDHD Level 1, insulin-dependent Type 2 diabetes, advanced peripheral neuropathy, Charcot foot, a collapsed diaphragm, gastroparesis, tinnitus, frozen shoulders on both sides, and colon cancer in remission. These can make commuting, long onsite days, frequent movement, prolonged standing, rigid schedules, noisy environments, and physically demanding workplace logistics more costly.

Remote work reduces avoidable movement, commute strain, sensory load, and interruption load. It helps preserve energy for the actual work rather than spending it on onsite logistics.

Hybrid work can be workable when onsite expectations are predictable, limited, and clearly worth the physical cost. Fully onsite work is higher-friction, especially when it requires long commutes, frequent walking between locations, standing, stairs, rigid break timing, noisy or crowded environments, or constant live interaction.

However, he does not treat remote work as an absolute requirement. He is open to hybrid roles and willing to consider relocation for a sufficiently strong, high-upside opportunity where the role, mission, degree of ownership, and practical accommodations justify the cost.
